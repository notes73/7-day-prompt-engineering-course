Day 7: Negotiation & Closing Deals

In this final stage, you'll learn how to negotiate effectively and close the deal with confidence. Our focus is on two key elements: asking for 20% above the initial offer and sealing the deal with a clear commitment.

Negotiation: Ask for 20% Above the Initial Offer

When you receive an offer, it's essential to know your worth. By asking for around 20% above the initial offer, you create room for negotiation while reflecting the value you bring. This strategy is based on thorough market research and a strong value proposition.

Key Strategies

  • Market Research: Understand salary benchmarks in your industry and role.
  • Value Proposition: Clearly articulate your unique skills and accomplishments.
  • Confidence: Approach negotiations assertively while remaining professional.

Example Negotiation Dialogue

Interviewer: "Our offer is $80,000 per year."
Candidate: "Based on my experience and the value I can add, I was expecting an offer closer to $96,000 per year. Could we discuss this further?"

Seal the Deal: Be Confident and Ready to Accept

Once you've reached an agreement, it's time to finalize the deal. Express your excitement and readiness to join the team by sending a clear confirmation. This step not only secures your position but also demonstrates your professionalism and enthusiasm.

Key Strategies

  • Clear Confirmation: Summarize the agreed terms in your follow-up communication.
  • Express Enthusiasm: Show your excitement about the role and the company.
  • Professional Closure: Confirm all details, such as start date, salary, and benefits.

Example Confirmation Email

Subject: Acceptance of Offer - [Position Title]

Hi [Hiring Manager Name],

I am thrilled to accept the offer for the [Position Title] role at [Company Name]. Thank you for this opportunity. I look forward to contributing to the team and starting on [Start Date].

Best regards,
[Your Name]
